Lasagna Recipe

Lasagna, lasagna. How I love thee! It is possibly one of the most loved foods in the whole wide world, and understandably so. There is just something so sentimental about lasagna, so comforting. It evokes images of of gatherings with family and friends all around the world. It is the sort of food that is like a big warm hug, and so more-ish you want to keep digging in until you burst. Lasagna just rocks. Full stop. And if you’ve never tried a homemade one before, that needs to change! If you can make spaghetti bolognese, you can make lasagne. It just requires a wee bit more patience. OK, bit more than a wee bit more patience. But it’s totally worth it. A real homemade Lasagna is epic.

Components

  • Meat Sauce
  • White Sauce
  • Assembly

What Goes In Lasagna

The Meat Sauce is just a bolognese. You'll need:

  • Onion, garlic, carrot, celery
  • Soffrito
  • Beef
  • Canned tomato and tomato paste
  • Dry red wine
  • Seasonings - Beef bouillon cubes, bay leaves, thyme, oregano, Worcestershire sauce

The White Sauce. You'll need

  • Butter
  • Flour
  • Milk
  • Cheese

The Assembly Process. You'll need

  • Lasagna sheets
  • Cheese

Making Your Lasagna

Meat (Bolognese) sauce

  1. Saute your onions and garlics till aromatic
  2. Add your beef and brown them. Drain most (not all) of the juices
  3. Add everything else and mix well
  4. Let simmer for a minimum of 20 minutes

White Sauce

  1. Melt the butter with the cheese
  2. Add the milk and mix
  3. Slowly add flour to desired consistency

Assembling

  1. Smear yo meat at the bottom of a ramekin
  2. Top it with your meat sauce and a bit of white sauce
  3. Lay out a sheet
  4. Repeat steps 1-3 until you want/have to stop
  5. Top that bad boy with the rest of your white sauce and sprinkle with cheese
  6. Pop it in the oven
